Major Projects Game

June 2022

ABOUT THIS PIECE

My major project for semester 1 was a video game and console, reminiscent of the Nintendo DS. The game would be of the exploration genre, featuring physical representations of my work. For instance, photography would be displayed in a gallery, while the Cyberthug Core from 2021 would be life-size with fancy effects.

I had planned to build the console using Raspberry Pi along with a combination of lasercutting and 3D printing techniques. The operating system for the console would be a stripped down version of Ubuntu with a custom made homescreen, however due to time limits I wasn't able to complete this.

NZ Final Drives

October 2023

ABOUT THIS PIECE

This job involved improving SEO visibility, performance and user-friendliness of NZ Final Drives' website. I found the old one to contain a lot of redundant information, such as the presence of a contact form right next to the company's phone numbers and email address.

The old website featured a products page where customers could search for parts, but ultimately everything lead to contacting the company for quotes. I figured it was appropriate to reduce everything to a single page, with the product finder moved to a dialog at the top of the page.
